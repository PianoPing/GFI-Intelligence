GL Diagnostic Reports — Sample Cases
Four systems, four diagnoses
Each report uses only public administrative data. No internal access required. These are sample diagnostics — illustrative of the GL Framework's analytical depth and output format.
🇺🇸 United States · Massachusetts
Department of Unemployment Assistance (DUA)
0.059
Flow Collapse
For every $1 of policy intent, less than 6 cents reaches eligible claimants as timely benefits. A 70,000+ case backlog and 9× surge in appeals are denominator failures — not technology failures. The system was built by a major integrator. The denominator problem it failed to address is exactly what GL measures.
GL = (0.44 × 1.2) / (3.0 × 3.0) = 0.059

🇬🇧 United Kingdom
Dept. for Work and Pensions — Universal Credit
0.074
Structural Failure
£22.7 billion in benefits go unclaimed annually. The 5-week structural wait, managed migration opt-in burden, and 110,000+ reassessment backlog are architectural failures. Infrastructure was modernized. The claimant-facing friction layer was not redesigned.
GL = (0.48 × 1.3) / (2.8 × 3.0) = 0.074
↓ Download Report
🇹🇼 Taiwan
Long-Term Care Plan 3.0 (長照3.0)
0.148
Structural Failure
Budget tripled 2017–2023. A major 2026 policy overhaul launched. Delivery efficiency remains below 15%. The front-loaded sequential assessment structure — not funding levels — is the primary friction source. Service volume expanded. The denominator did not shrink.
GL = (0.54 × 1.35) / (2.5 × 2.7) = 0.148
↓ Download Report
🇺🇸 United States · Iowa
Iowa Medicaid Eligibility System
0.082
Structural Failure
System operated by a major contractor under a $60M+ contract. 72% of 247,522 disenrollments were procedural — the system lacked data, not eligibility evidence. Ex parte renewal rate of 46% lagged the 60% national average. Every new policy layer costs $20M+ to implement. The denominator architecture is the problem.
GL = (0.46 × 1.4) / (2.6 × 3.0) = 0.082
