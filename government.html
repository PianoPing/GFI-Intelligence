Why it matters

Why public agencies use GL

Delivery problems are often discussed as funding shortages or staffing strain. GL adds another view: whether the process itself is creating avoidable friction that weakens outcomes.

Cross-jurisdiction comparison

See how similar processes perform across agencies, offices, or states using a common structural lens.

No system exposure

Analysis can begin from public, exported, or manually assembled operational data without internal system access.

Leadership-ready framing

Produce a clearer discussion of delivery burden, delay, and process failure without turning the exercise into blame assignment.

Start here

Start with 3 fields. That’s it.

You can begin with only three operational inputs for an initial directional diagnostic.

1

Total applications
(or cases)

How many people entered the process in the period being measured.

2

Average processing time

Days or hours from submission to determination, approval, or next actionable step.

3

Drop-off / appeal / manual review rate

The share of cases that did not complete smoothly, required rework, escalated, or moved into review.

Estimates are acceptable for an initial directional diagnostic. You can start with one program, one unit, or one workflow.
